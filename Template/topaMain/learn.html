Sustainability

DEADSTOCK FABRICS
. We only use deadstock fabrics to create our collection. Nepal is geographically close to India, China, and Bangladesh, which are among the largest garment producers in the world and the deadstock fabrics of these countries are often passed on to the Nepali market. We utilize these fabrics to produce our designs.

CLEAN, RENEWABLE ENERGY
As TŌPA’s manufacturing unit is in Kathmandu, Nepal, our source of electricity is hydropower energy. All of our machines are operated on clean and renewable energy.

LEAN PRODUCTION
For every collection, TŌPA produces only a limited number of pieces per design. A lean production model ensures high quality is maintained and excessive number of garments are not produced.

SUSTAINABLE PACKAGING
All of TŌPA’s packaging materials are made from bio-degradable materials. Our packaging envelope is made from Lokta paper, which is handmade from the bark and bush of the lokta plant which regenerates every 4 - 5 years.

Ethical Values

Ethical Values
. Fair Remunation for Workers We pay our workers 163% more than the government-recommended minimum wage. Realizing that the minimum wage set by the government is not enough to live a healthy quality of life, we have set our own minimum wage rate for our staƋ by discussing it with them. We review the wage rate yearly for all our employees.

Safe & Comfortable Working ConditionsOur manufacturing facility is designed considering the safety and comfort of our workers. We provide ergonomic chairs for all our employees and protective gear which ensures their well-being.

Transparent Sourcing & OperationWe provide all the information regarding the sources of the fabrics used for our garment production on our website. We also provide details about how we operate and what processes a garment goes through during production. Additionally, anyone can freely send us an email at info@topanewyork.com if they want to know any information regarding the brand.
